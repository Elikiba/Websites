The first order of business was to get 2 of Uniben's finest barbers (who were far better than myself at the time - I learned from them!) as partners, then we employed a system of Reverse Psychology to attract more customers, by increasing our price above the average then, together with the superb skill of my 2 guys, Ola & Emmie, sales took off! My NYSC Story: Abia 2 Lagos

I was deployed to do my NYSC in Abia state. This spoiled my plan of getting access to various educational and financial opportunities in Lagos. I redeployed but was posted to Ondo state, which was less preferable to Abia state. That worsened everything. Money, time, effort, wasted. Everyone suggested that I give up - "just accept where you were posted, after all, you've tried your best", "don't bother yourself, it'll be fine". I've never believed in the concept of 'que sera sera'; I believe that if it's going to happen, I'm going to have to make it happen. I documented at Ondo State, borrowed some money, and reapplied for relocation. I searched painstakingly for a favorable establishment and served committedly while awaiting the confirmation of my redeployment. I wrote out a list of possible outcomes of the search for redeployment, from the most to the least desirable. “Why not just relax and settle down where you are? Save yourself the stress” These are comments I received from well-meaning people. Additionally, every redeployed corper I knew who was posted to a different state from where they wished either settled there or returned to Abia state. I, on the other hand, did not get a reply for weeks. After 2 months, it was already late. I was to remain in Ondo state. The point of this story is that I did not feel bad or unhappy about it; I was at peace because I knew I GENUINELY tried my best. And so I kept my head down and sought positives. It was good to confirm my GRIT and RESILIENCE. On the sixth day of the third month, however, my redeployment finally went through. I settled everything at Ondo and left for Lagos a week later.
